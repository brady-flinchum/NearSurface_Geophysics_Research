Welcome
-------

I am a near‑surface geophysicist who uses geophysical methods to characterise the critical zone (CZ)—the layer of the Earth that extends from treetops to bedrock and supports all terrestrial life. The CZ, where regolith forms, is inherently interdisciplinary and draws together researchers from across the Earth sciences. My research focuses on bridging gaps between measurements that span wide temporal and spatial scales, using the unique imaging capabilities of geophysics to develop new perspectives on the physical and chemical processes that create regolith. I specialise in shallow seismic refraction, but I also have experience with ground‑penetrating radar (GPR), seismic reflection, direct current (DC) electrical resistivity, and surface and borehole nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR).
Since beginning my PhD in 2013, I have been deeply committed to mentoring undergraduate and graduate students. I value students’ perspectives, time, and life commitments, and I strive to foster a supportive and safe working environment. I believe that meaningful research requires passion, curiosity, and resilience, and I openly share my technical knowledge to help students reach their full potential. Safety and equity—particularly in field‑based Earth science research—are central to my mentoring and teaching philosophy.
As geoscientists, I believe we work in the realm of the unknown, constantly pushing to expand our understanding of Earth’s complex systems. Many of the research challenges we face demand novel, creative, and sometimes audacious solutions. I view diversity and multiculturalism as fundamental to creating inclusive, innovative environments where new ideas can flourish. I deeply value the perspectives that emerge from diverse teams and organisations, as they are essential to both successful research and effective teaching. I make a conscious effort to listen to many viewpoints, recognising that everyone brings experiences that can shape our values and ways of thinking. By listening to one another, I believe we become better researchers, teachers, students, and human beings. To me, diversity extends beyond race, gender, and culture to include differences in working styles and preferences, such as being introverted or extroverted. Although building and sustaining a healthy, diverse environment requires collective effort, the result is a space where creative, innovative, and imaginative ideas can truly thrive.
